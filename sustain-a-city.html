01. Overview

The Project

Sustainacity is a unique superhero game developed in Unreal Engine 5 with a core theme of sustainability. Designed during my third year of college, this project prioritized documentation and theoretical application over complex mechanics.

You play as a superhero flying through a procedurally generated low-poly city, tasked with defending it from incoming asteroids using laser-eye abilities. The project combines smooth flight mechanics with arcade-style defence gameplay.

02. Research & Theory

The Science of Engagement

My dissertation, "How do you maintain engagement in a simulation game?", formed the theoretical backbone of this project. I explored strategies to sustain player interest through difficulty adjustment and immersion.

Theory

Dynamic Difficulty

Kristan et al.

Adjusting the game's challenge level in real-time based on player performance to maintain the "Flow" state‚Äîpreventing boredom or frustration.

Psychology

Autotelic Personality

Flow Psychology

Designing for players who are self-motivated by the activity itself. Creating systems that reward curiosity and mastery of complex mechanics.

Design

Immersion

Michailidis et al.

Creating authentic environments and narrative depth to motivate exploration. If the world feels real, players are more likely to care about saving it.

03. Development

Building the Simulation

Core Systems

While the focus was on documentation, I implemented several key systems to create a functional prototype. The procedural city generation ensures a unique environment for every playthrough, while the flight controller provides a satisfying sense of movement.

  • Procedural City WFC algorithm generates unique low-poly layouts.
  • Flight Mechanics Smooth superhero flight controls.
  • Laser Combat Auto-aim assist for destroying asteroids.

Tools & Tech

Unreal Engine 5

Utilized Blueprints for rapid prototyping of simulation logic and the UI system.

Blender

Created a cohesive Low Poly art style to allow for large-scale city rendering without performance hits.

C++ / Blueprints

Hybrid workflow using C++ for core systems performance and Blueprints for rapid gameplay iteration.

Canva

Used for planning layouts, presentation slides, and visual documentation.

04. Reflection

Post-Mortem

What Went Well

  • Efficient Documentation: Maintained comprehensive documentation of development progress over several months.
  • Technical Implementation: Created efficient blueprints and animation work that streamlined development.
  • Gameplay Validation: Achieved a sufficient and enjoyable gameplay result confirmed through playtesting.

Areas for Improvement

  • Visual Fidelity: Could have created better graphics, including more realistic buildings and a larger city scale.
  • Mechanics Refinement: The gameplay mechanics and difficulty tuning could have been more refined to enhance player engagement.
